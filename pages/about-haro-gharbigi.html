About Haro Gharbigi

Tech entrepreneur & healthcare AI innovator

Haro Gharbigi (also known as Gharbigi Haro) is a Los Angeles-based technology entrepreneur dedicated to transforming healthcare through artificial intelligence. As the founder and CEO of AxiaASC, he builds intelligent software solutions that help ambulatory surgery centers operate more efficiently while maintaining the highest standards of patient care.

Background & Experience

With nearly two decades of entrepreneurial experience, Haro has built multiple successful technology ventures across diverse industries. His career spans e-commerce, digital marketing, SaaS development, and healthcare technology. This breadth of experience informs his practical approach to solving complex business problems with technology.

Before founding AxiaASC, Haro held roles in business development and performance marketing, gaining deep expertise in customer acquisition, conversion optimization, and building scalable systems. These skills translate directly into creating software that healthcare professionals actually want to use.

Current Focus: Healthcare AI

Today, Haro's work centers on applying artificial intelligence to healthcare. AxiaASC targets ambulatory surgery centers (ASCs), a growing but underserved segment of healthcare delivery. These facilities perform millions of outpatient procedures annually but often lack the sophisticated software tools available to larger hospital systems.

AxiaASC addresses this gap with AI-powered scheduling optimization, predictive analytics for resource allocation, and intelligent workflow automation. The goal is always practical: help healthcare professionals spend less time on administrative tasks and more time caring for patients.

Based in Los Angeles

Haro operates from Los Angeles, California, positioning AxiaASC at the heart of one of America's largest healthcare markets. Southern California's high concentration of ambulatory surgery centers provides direct market access and enables close relationships with the healthcare professionals who use the software daily.

Philosophy

Haro believes that the best technology is invisible technology—solutions that work seamlessly in the background, making complex processes simple without requiring a PhD to operate. In healthcare, this means building AI that augments human judgment rather than replacing it, providing better data and insights that empower clinicians to make better decisions.
